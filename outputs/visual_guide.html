1. What is the Digital Divide?

Fast Internet Home

  • Videos play instantly
  • Video calls are smooth
  • Downloads take seconds
  • Online homework is easy
  • Parents can work from home

Slow Internet Home

  • Videos buffer constantly
  • Video calls freeze/drop
  • Downloads take hours
  • Online homework frustrating
  • Work from home impossible

The Digital Divide = The gap between these two experiences

It's not just about convenience - it affects education, jobs, healthcare, and opportunity.

4. Key Discoveries

Discovery #1: FCC Data is Wrong

What FCC Says:

100%

of locations have 25 Mbps available

Reality (Ookla):

12.4%

of counties have speeds BELOW 25 Mbps

Worst Examples: Keya Paha County, NE has 100% "coverage" but only 0.4 Mbps actual speed!

Discovery #2: Rural America is Left Behind

Area Type Avg Download % Below 25 Mbps
Metro (Cities) 110 Mbps 3.9%
Near Cities 82 Mbps 2.8%
Remote Rural 52 Mbps 25.1%
Key Finding: Rural areas are 6x more likely to have inadequate internet!

Discovery #3: Big Cities Have Big Numbers Too

Our top priority counties are large metro areas because they have the most TOTAL underserved people:

6. Glossary

Technical terms explained simply:

Term Simple Explanation
Broadband Fast internet (at least 25 Mbps download speed)
Mbps Megabits per second - how we measure internet speed. Higher = faster.
Download Speed How fast you can GET stuff from the internet (watching videos, loading pages)
Upload Speed How fast you can SEND stuff to the internet (video calls, uploading photos)
Latency The delay when data travels. Lower is better. Measured in milliseconds (ms).
FCC Federal Communications Commission - the government agency that regulates internet
FIPS Code A 5-digit ID number for each county (like 06037 = Los Angeles)
SVI Social Vulnerability Index - measures how vulnerable a community is (0-1 scale)
CDVI Our custom score for digital vulnerability - higher means more help needed
Rural Areas far from cities with few people - often have worse internet
Regression A math method to find what factors predict an outcome
R / RStudio Programming language and software we use for the analysis

Remember the Key Takeaways!

  1. FCC data overstates coverage - reality is different
  2. Rural areas are 6x more likely to have bad internet
  3. Vulnerable communities get hit hardest
  4. Big cities have many underserved people by total numbers
  5. Regional approaches work best - nearby counties cluster
